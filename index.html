The Problem

Why today's learning
isn't working.

Students across the world face a broken learning loop — they absorb content passively but never truly internalize it. The system rewards memorization, not mastery. Three deep cracks run through it.

📚

Passive Learning Trap

Conventional learning is constrained by marks. Students study to pass, not to understand — optimizing for tests, not for knowledge that actually sticks.

🐢

Inefficient Group Study

Group studies are slow and unstructured. Students are rarely taught how to learn — only what to learn. Most sessions generate the feeling of productivity without the substance.

📵

Zero Accountability

Learners are flooded with distractions in the online age — no genuine mechanism to keep accountability. Without a real external commitment, motivation collapses.

Learning Science

The Feynman Technique

Nobel Prize-winning physicist Richard Feynman discovered that you only truly understand something when you can explain it simply to someone else. If you can't, you have a gap — and teaching immediately surfaces exactly where that gap is. FeynLearn's entire session structure is built on this loop.

Psychology

Albert Bandura's Social Learning Theory

Bandura proved that humans learn most powerfully through social interaction — not isolated study. When peers teach peers, both parties engage deeply. The teacher organizes their knowledge; the learner receives it from someone uniquely relatable. FeynLearn's peer model is grounded entirely in this research.
